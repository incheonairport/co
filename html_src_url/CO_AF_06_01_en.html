Energy Management Policy

In order to reach the vision of “2nd Flight as the Global Leader Airport”, Incheon International Airport Corporation has set a strategy of “Manifestation of a green airport” and is tirelessly working to set-up and maintain the Environment Management System to minimize the environmental effect of airport operation and construction. We propose the following policies for this purpose.

  • Abide by international and domestic energy laws and treaties, and work to promote energy management and share the outcomes internally and externally.
  • Purchase and use energy conservation productions to fulfill social responsibility as a airport corporation.
  • Expand actively use of renewable energy in airport management and construction to increase energy independence and decrease fossil fuel use with the ultimate goal of lowering greenhouse gas emission.
  • Adopt and enact environmental goals and improvement plans and operate a proactive and strategic Environment Management System through sustained outcome review.

Greenhouse Gas Emission Reduction and Energy Conservation Committee

Incheon International Airport has formed a committee to more effectively and efficiently conserve energy and reduce greenhouse gas emission. The Committee’s main functions are as follows:

  1. 1. Adoption and implementation of low-emission, environment-friendly strategy and basic plan
  2. 2. Analysis and evaluation of low-emission, environment-friendly strategy efforts and outcomes
  3. 3. Responses to climate change to reduce emission and conserve energy
  4. 4. Training and promotion of low-emission, environment-friendly works
  5. 5. All other matters that the Committee Chair deems relevant to sustainable growth as a low-emission, environment-friendly corporation

Environment Management System (ISO 50001) Certification

Beginning October 2010, Incheon International Airport Corporation has participated in the “Environment Management System Certification Program” operated by the Korea Energy Agency to continually improve energy management capacity.
In February 2012, we became the first airport in the world to receive the ISO 50001 certification in the building category, and even after the official ISO 50001 certification for management systems in 2014, we receive yearly recertification to meet the international standard.

Airports Council International (ACI) Airport Carbon Accreditation

Incheon International Airport Corporation was awarded the Level 3 Airport Carbon Accreditation, the highest standard in Asia, based on its work to respond to climate change. Incheon Airport was the third large-sized airport in Asia (after Hong Kong’s Chek Lap Kok Airport and India’s New Delhi Airport) to receive Level 3 Airport Carbon Accreditation, which also is an internal accreditation that 40 airports around the world, including Heathrow, Munich, LA, and Zurich Airports, have.
