2001: Opening of Incheon International Airport, 101 months of grand history

Incheon International Airport construction project in an effort to build a 24-hour East Asia hub airport and respond to the steep growth of airport demand. Despite advice that it is difficult, even impossible, Yeongjongdo Island was deemed the ideal location, and with the groundbreaking in 1992, a new history was written. The most advanced construction methods and IT system were incorporated in building the airport facility. The national project that took astronomically number of people and equipment was completed 85 months after the site building work (101 months total) on March 29, 2001 as the first of its kind, and it has become the pride of the country.

2008: acclaimed as the top-tier airport of the world

To gain a competitive advantage in response to exponential growth of airport demand by building the East Asia’s hub airport, Stage 2 construction began in 2002. The focus of the construction was 4,000m runways for aircrafts, cargo terminal, concourse, and other state-of-the-art facilities required for a global transit hub. Stage 2 was completed in 2008, which became the stepping stone for future growth of the top-tier airport with high-tech system and service.

2018: a 2nd leap through the construction of a luxury airport

Despite hardships and severe conditions after the opening, Incheon International Airport has grown to be number 8 in the world for number of passengers and number 3 in the world for cargo load. To continue this steady development, Stage 3 construction, begun in 2009, is full of will and vision of a 2nd opening of a world-class luxury airport. Upon completion of Stage 3, which included Passenger Terminal 2, Transit Center 2, railroad tracks, road, and other transportation access system, Incheon International Airport became the global hub airport that can accommodate 72 million passengers and 5.8 million ton of cargo yearly.
